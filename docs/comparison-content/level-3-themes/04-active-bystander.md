# Theme 4 — Active-bystander skills and anti-discrimination

How RSHE, Welsh CfW, CASEL, and REAL each treat the territory that REAL holds across LTs 4.3, 4.1, 5.1, and 1.2.

---

Active-bystander capability sits at the intersection of empathy, boundaries, communication, and discrimination-recognition. In REAL it spans four learning targets: bystander analysis and anti-discrimination (LT 4.3), bodily autonomy and consent including power dynamics (LT 4.1), interpersonal communication including disagreement (LT 5.1), and reading others' emotional states with repair after conflict (LT 1.2). Each external framework names some of these elements, in different shapes.

RSHE addresses the territory directly across respectful-relationships content. At primary, pupils learn "that each person's body belongs to them, and the differences between appropriate and inappropriate or unsafe contact" [rshe-pilot-extracts §29] and "how to recognise when a relationship is harmful or dangerous" [rshe-pilot-extracts §30]. At secondary, the framework names "the responsibilities of bystanders to report bullying and how and where to get help" [rshe-pilot-extracts §33] and addresses stereotypes "based on sex, gender reassignment, race, religion, sexual orientation or disability" [rshe-pilot-extracts §35]. The framing is recognition-and-reporting; the bystander's role is to identify and route to help.

In Welsh CfW, the territory is distributed across Health and Well-being's What Matters statements and the separate RSE Code. WM4 builds the social-shaping arc from PS3's "rules, norms and behaviours… have an influence on me" [cfw-pilot-extracts §22] to PS5's "promote positive attitudes and values, and…challenge harmful ones" [cfw-pilot-extracts §24]. The RSE Code lists bullying-reduction as a protective outcome of high-quality RSE, including "help reduce all bullying, including homophobic, biphobic and transphobic bullying" [cfw-rse-statutory-principles §4]. Bullying is not named within the Health and Well-being Area's What Matters statements directly.

CASEL routes bystander territory through Social Awareness and Relationship Skills. At Grade 4–5, students "develop an understanding and appreciation of different abilities, backgrounds, cultures and social groups" and "explain the importance of having empathy for others and their perspectives" [casel-pilot-extracts §18]; at Grade 6–8, they "define stereotyping, discrimination, and prejudice and understand how they can be harmful" [casel-pilot-extracts §19]; at Grade 9–10, they "recognise and describe unfairness and injustice in many forms including attitudes, speech, behaviors, policies, practices, and laws" [casel-pilot-extracts §26]. Relationship Skills at Grade 4–5 carries the conflict-resolution thread: "demonstrate positive and respectful communication" and "practice resolving conflicts with peers independently and/or with adult support" [casel-pilot-extracts §23].

REAL distributes the territory across four learning targets and integrates them at Band F of LT 4.3. LT 1.2 reaches Band D where the teacher notices the student "after a conflict, listening without interrupting, naming their own contribution to the situation, and proposing a next step that accounts for the other person's position" [real-framework-overview §25]. LT 4.1 names power-dynamic content at Band D — "Power imbalance occurs when one person has more age, status, role authority, peer-group standing, or control over outcomes than the other" [real-framework-overview §43]. LT 5.1 builds calibrated communication for disagreement contexts. LT 4.3 reaches Band D where the student writes a bystander analysis identifying "the forms of discrimination present, names the specific group and power dynamics at play, and justifies a proposed response" [real-framework-overview §46], and Band F where the student analyses a real bystander success or failure and proposes "specific group, school, or community-level conditions that would change the outcome next time" [real-framework-overview §47].

Bystander capability is where four threads meet — empathy, boundaries, communication, and discrimination-recognition. RSHE develops each thread separately and names the bystander role as recognition-and-reporting. CfW splits the territory between Health and Well-being's social-influences arc and the separate RSE Code. CASEL builds it through Social Awareness's recognition content and Relationship Skills' conflict-resolution content. REAL synthesises the four threads at Band F of LT 4.3, where the student is asked to analyse a real situation, name the conditions that allowed it, and propose what would change the outcome next time.
