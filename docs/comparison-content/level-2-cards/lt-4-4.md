# LT 4.4 — Emotional Development & Relationships

REAL learning target description: use knowledge of emotional and relational development to identify, explain, and analyse patterns in emotional experiences and relationships. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

Pupils studying secondary RSHE learn to talk about emotions accurately, recognise that worrying and feeling down are normal, and understand common types of mental ill health [rshe-pilot-extracts §11, §12, §13]. REAL covers adjacent territory but adds the science: students at Bands D and above learn dual-process accounts of emotion, the stress-response window, habit formation, and the neurobiology of attraction, rejection and loss, and use those frameworks to analyse patterns in described relationships [real-framework-overview §14, §15]. RSHE doesn't reach that explanatory framing.

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

At Progression Step 5, CfW asks learners to "show an understanding of the complex nature of relationships in a range of contexts" [cfw-pilot-extracts §16], and at earlier steps to develop empathy and regulate emotions [cfw-what-matters §8]. What CfW does not include is the biological and psychological material REAL builds in at Bands D–F: dual-process accounts of emotion, attachment theory, the neuroscience of attraction and loss [real-framework-overview §14, §15, §16]. CfW treats relationships as something to learn through; REAL also treats them as something to study.

---

## Comparison with CASEL

CASEL's five competencies cover the practice of relationships, self-management of emotions, and decision-making [casel-framework-definition §1, §3]. The biology and psychology of emotional development sit outside the framework — CASEL doesn't specify mechanism-level content. REAL's LT 4.4 adds that explanatory layer at Bands D–F: dual-process accounts of emotion, attachment theory, and the neuroscience of attraction, rejection and loss, applied to real or described relationships [real-framework-overview §14, §15, §16]. A CASEL-based programme covers the relational practice but not the underlying science.
