# LT 2.1 — Focused Attention & Strategy

REAL learning target description: apply attention strategies to sustain focus, identify what supports or disrupts attention, and evaluate strategies across contexts. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

RSHE's mental-wellbeing curriculum focuses on naming feelings, judging whether they're proportionate, and seeking support [rshe-foreword §1, rshe-pilot-extracts §2, §3]. Attention as a topic doesn't appear; the curriculum has no progression for sustaining focus or designing conditions for it. REAL's LT 2.1 builds a continuous strand from Band A: students apply attention strategies, identify what disrupts focus, and at upper bands design conditions for sustained attention in their own work, including managing digital distractions [real-framework-overview §31].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

Welsh CfW's mental-wellbeing statement names "focus my attention" at PS1 and "self-awareness" through later steps [cfw-pilot-extracts §1, §3], but doesn't progress to a place where students design and test their own attention strategies. REAL's LT 2.1 specifies that work across Bands A–F: at Band E, students "design the conditions for sustained attention in a piece of my own work — including managing digital distractions — and produce a written evaluation of how different attentional strategies interacted with different types of tasks" [real-framework-overview §31].

---

## Comparison with CASEL

Persistence shows up in CASEL's Self-Management. At Grade 6–8 students "practice various coping skills to process thoughts and manage stressful situations" and "reframe a challenging situation into a positive learning opportunity for growth" [casel-pilot-extracts §22]. REAL's LT 2.1 takes adjacent territory further at upper bands: at Band F, students audit their own attentional patterns across high-stakes work and propose specific adjustments to their workflow [real-framework-overview §32]. CASEL covers persistence in the moment; REAL teaches students to design how their own attention works.
