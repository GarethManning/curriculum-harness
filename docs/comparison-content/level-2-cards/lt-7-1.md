# LT 7.1 — Pattern Analysis & Adjustment

REAL learning target description: notice patterns in thinking or behaviour, analyse what drives them, design targeted adjustments, and build a structured personal metacognitive practice. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

RSHE doesn't address metacognition. The closest territory it covers is mental-wellbeing language — recognising and naming emotions, judging whether feelings are appropriate and proportionate [rshe-pilot-extracts §3] — but pattern-noticing about thinking itself isn't part of the statutory specification. REAL's LT 7.1 develops this directly from Band A onward: students notice patterns in their own thinking and behaviour, analyse what drives them, design targeted adjustments, and build a structured personal metacognitive practice [real-framework-overview §17, §19].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

At Progression Step 4, CfW asks learners to "research, examine and evaluate a range of evidence to make considered and informed decisions" [cfw-pilot-extracts §10] and at Step 5 to "critically evaluate factors and implications, including risks" [cfw-pilot-extracts §13]. REAL's LT 7.1 starts from a different angle — pattern-noticing about one's own thinking — and continues into a structured personal metacognitive framework at Bands E–F [real-framework-overview §17, §19]. CfW's reflective decision-making is adjacent territory but not the same skill.

---

## Comparison with CASEL

Reflection appears across CASEL's Self-Awareness and Responsible Decision-Making competencies: at Grade 6–8 students "reflect on personal behaviors and choices and identify what was helpful versus harmful" [casel-pilot-extracts §5] and "utilize the steps of a decision-making model" [casel-pilot-extracts §10]. REAL's LT 7.1 develops a different practice — analysing one's own patterns of thinking, designing targeted adjustments, and building a named personal metacognitive framework [real-framework-overview §17, §19]. CASEL covers reflection on choices; REAL covers reflection on the thinking that produces them.
