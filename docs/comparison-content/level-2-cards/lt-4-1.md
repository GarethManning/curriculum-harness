# LT 4.1 — Bodies, Boundaries & Consent

REAL learning target description: recognise and respect bodily autonomy, apply consent principles across everyday, sexual, and online contexts, and analyse power dynamics. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

On safeguarding specificity at primary, RSHE does this directly. Pupils learn "each person's body belongs to them, and the differences between appropriate and inappropriate or unsafe contact" [rshe-pilot-extracts §29] and "how to recognise when a relationship is harmful or dangerous" [rshe-pilot-extracts §30]. For young children, this terminal-knowledge specificity is exactly what protects them — and RSHE's statutory floor locks it at age. REAL's LT 4.1 includes the same primary safeguarding content within a broader consent arc to Band F [real-framework-overview §41, §42].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

CfW addresses bodily-safety territory through WM5 — "I can recognise when relationships are unhealthy and need to be aware of how to keep safe, and seek support for themselves and others" [cfw-pilot-extracts §15]. The framing is healthy-relationships generally; consent as an explicit construct sits in the separate RSE Code [cfw-rse-statutory-principles §5]. REAL's LT 4.1 names consent directly: at Band B, what a clear request and clear answer sound like; at Band D, analysis of power imbalance in pressure scenarios [real-framework-overview §42, §43].

---

## Comparison with CASEL

CASEL's Relationship Skills at G4–5 covers "demonstrate positive and respectful communication" and "practice resolving conflicts with peers independently and/or with adult support" [casel-pilot-extracts §23]. The framing is positive communication and conflict resolution; explicit consent as an analytical construct doesn't appear. REAL's LT 4.1 develops consent specifically: at Band B, identifying whether consent was "freely given, unclear, or overridden by pressure" [real-framework-overview §42]; at Band F, surfacing and correcting common consent myths [real-framework-overview §44].
