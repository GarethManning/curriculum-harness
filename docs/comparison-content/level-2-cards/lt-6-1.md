# LT 6.1 — Brain, Body & Wellbeing Science

REAL learning target description: explain stress, emotion, attention, and habit mechanisms using accurate vocabulary, and evaluate claims about brain and wellbeing interventions. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

RSHE addresses mental wellbeing and the changing adolescent body. Pupils learn that "worrying and feeling down are normal" and not a sign of mental ill-health [rshe-pilot-extracts §12], and learn "the facts about puberty, the changing adolescent body, including brain development" [rshe-pilot-extracts §14]. REAL's LT 6.1 sequences neuroscience vocabulary at mechanism level: fight-flight-freeze and the amygdala at Band B; HPA axis, prefrontal cortex, and dopamine at Band D; a Band F critical essay evaluating a contested wellbeing-science claim [real-framework-overview §58, §59, §60].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

CfW's WM2 develops emotional self-regulation in plain language: at PS3, "self-regulate my emotions in a healthy way using strategies that I have developed" [cfw-pilot-extracts §3]; at PS5, "use my self-awareness to appreciate the complexity of my emotions and apply strategies to self-regulate them" [cfw-pilot-extracts §5]. What REAL's LT 6.1 adds is the mechanism vocabulary itself — amygdala, prefrontal cortex, HPA axis, dopamine — used to explain why those strategies work, and at Band F to evaluate wellbeing-science claims with them [real-framework-overview §59, §60].

---

## Comparison with CASEL

Coping behaviour is the focus of CASEL's Self-Management at upper bands: G4–5 "use different strategies to self-regulate" [casel-pilot-extracts §1]; G6–8 "practice various coping skills to process thoughts and manage stressful situations" [casel-pilot-extracts §6]. REAL's LT 6.1 builds the mechanism-level vocabulary that explains why coping works — the threat-response pathway, the HPA axis, dopamine anticipation, the habit loop — with Band F producing a critical essay on a contested claim using those mechanisms [real-framework-overview §58, §60].
