# LT 8.3 — Digital Assertiveness & Wellbeing Strategies

REAL learning target description: maintain healthy digital boundaries, communicate assertively online, and sustain wellbeing-supporting practices across platforms and contexts over time. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

RSHE addresses online safety from primary "how to recognise harmful content or harmful contact, and how to report this" [rshe-pilot-extracts §8] through to secondary content covering respectful online interactions, age restrictions, online abuse and bullying, and how to report concerns [rshe-pilot-extracts §21]. REAL's LT 8.3 covers similar safeguarding ground at Band B [real-framework-overview §74] and reaches Band F where digital life is treated as an expression of values — what the student engages with, creates, refuses — visible in how they behave online unprompted [real-framework-overview §73].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

Healthy relationships in CfW's WM5 run without a digital-specific subdivision. PS3 has learners communicate needs and feelings and identify conflict to resolve it [cfw-pilot-extracts §15]; PS5 reaches "meaningful connections with others, valuing safe, healthy and equitable relationships in a range of contexts" [cfw-pilot-extracts §16]. REAL's LT 8.3 names digital settings explicitly — boundaries kept under pressure at Band D, online life carried as a values expression by Band F [real-framework-overview §72, §73].

---

## Comparison with CASEL

CASEL's Relationship Skills includes digital settings within general communication content: at G6–8, students "demonstrate positive communication skills, including interactions on social and digital media, and reflect on the potential impact on their reputation and relationships" [casel-pilot-extracts §24]. REAL's LT 8.3 adds a sustained thread across platforms — at Band D, the student holds boundaries through social pressure; at Band F, digital life becomes the student's values made visible without performance [real-framework-overview §72, §73].
