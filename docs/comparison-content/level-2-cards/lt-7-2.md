# LT 7.2 — Self-Direction in Practice

REAL learning target description: consistently direct own thinking and learning — noticing, analysing, and adjusting patterns without external prompting across time and contexts. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

RSHE's foreword lists characteristics "including resilience, self-worth, self-respect, honesty, integrity, courage, kindness, and trustworthiness" as outcomes of effective teaching [rshe-foreword §1]. Metacognitive self-direction — noticing, analysing, and adjusting one's own patterns without external prompting — sits outside that scope. REAL's LT 7.2 is observation-driven: at Band D, the teacher notices the student articulating a specific adjustment they have made to their thinking, describing its effect across more than one domain, without prompting [real-framework-overview §66].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

CfW's progression model is the spiral revisit: each PS returns to the same constructs at deeper levels — at PS1, "I can notice and communicate how I am feeling" [cfw-pilot-extracts §2]; by PS5, the same emotional self-regulation construct returns deeper, applied with awareness of complexity [cfw-pilot-extracts §5]. That spiral is a framework-level structural feature. REAL's LT 7.2 has a Bands A–F arc but doesn't name spiral-revisit at the framework level — bands carry distinct content rather than revisiting one construct [real-framework-overview §10].

---

## Comparison with CASEL

Reflection on goal-progress is what CASEL's Self-Management asks for: at G6–8, "reflect on progress toward a goal and evaluate the steps needed to achieve this goal" [casel-pilot-extracts §7]; at G9–10, "monitor progress toward a specific goal by developing check-points or adjusting the plan as needed" [casel-pilot-extracts §13]. REAL's LT 7.2 reaches similar territory at Band D and extends to Band F: a stable, articulated way the student learns, reflects, and adjusts that holds in highly novel or unfamiliar contexts without scaffolding [real-framework-overview §8, §66].
