# LT 4.5 — Emotional Self-Management in Practice

REAL learning target description: manage emotional activation in practice — noticing it as it arises, using regulation strategies, seeking support when overwhelmed, and repairing relationships after conflict — consistently across contexts and with decreasing reliance on adult scaffolding over time. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

RSHE's secondary mental-wellbeing curriculum has pupils learn to talk about emotions and recognise that "worrying and feeling down are normal" and not in themselves a sign of mental ill-health [rshe-pilot-extracts §11, §12]. REAL's LT 4.5 stays on similar recognition ground but anchors progress in what the teacher sees: the student, across multiple emotional situations, using taught regulation strategies in practice and seeking support when approaching their limit [real-framework-overview §49].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

CfW reaches a place at PS5 that REAL doesn't: "I can contribute towards a culture where talking about mental health and emotional well-being is encouraged and normalised" [cfw-pilot-extracts §6]. That's a contribution-to-culture move — emotional literacy as a public, communal practice, not only a private regulation skill. REAL's LT 4.5 is observation-driven and individual: the teacher notices the student managing their own activation across contexts, decreasing reliance on adult scaffolding over time [real-framework-overview §48, §49]. Same territory, individual vs. cultural register.

---

## Comparison with CASEL

CASEL's Self-Management runs through similar content: at G4–5 "recognize when they are having strong emotions and use different strategies to self-regulate and respond to difficult situations" [casel-pilot-extracts §1]; at G6–8 "practice various coping skills" [casel-pilot-extracts §6]. REAL's LT 4.5 sits on the same arc, but the recording standard differs — strategy use is counted only when the teacher sees it in genuinely activated moments, not at-rehearsal demonstrations [real-framework-overview §49].
