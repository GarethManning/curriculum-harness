# LT 3.1 — Health Literacy & Habits

REAL learning target description: identify bodily needs, design and adjust health habits, and evaluate information quality and structural conditions shaping what is possible. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

At secondary, RSHE specifies physical health and healthy eating: "the characteristics of a healthy lifestyle, including physical activity and maintaining a healthy weight" and "how to maintain healthy eating and the links between a poor diet and health risks" [rshe-pilot-extracts §26, §27]. REAL's LT 3.1 starts at Band A and adds a structural-conditions layer at Band E: students identify social determinants — income, neighbourhood, food access, cultural norms — that shape what habits are realistically available [real-framework-overview §37].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

Worth saying plainly: on day-to-day physical literacy, Welsh CfW is more comprehensive than REAL. WM1 carries diet and physical activity through every Progression Step, from PS1's basic connections [cfw-pilot-extracts §17] to PS5's adjusted-for-context nutrition planning [cfw-pilot-extracts §21]. REAL's LT 3.1 adds a structural-factors lens at Bands E–F that CfW doesn't [real-framework-overview §37], but on practical physical literacy that families and primary teachers most use, CfW is more systematic.

---

## Comparison with CASEL

CASEL's five competencies cover self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship skills and decision-making [casel-framework-definition §3, §4]. Physical health habits — nutrition, sleep, movement — sit outside this scope. REAL's LT 3.1 develops both: at Band B, students set healthy-habit goals based on how sleep, food and movement affect how they feel; by Band E, they're evaluating health-claim sources alongside the social factors that shape habit access [real-framework-overview §37].
