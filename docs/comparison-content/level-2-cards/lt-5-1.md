# LT 5.1 — Interpersonal Skills & Communication

REAL learning target description: communicate clearly across contexts — including disagreement — by listening actively, calibrating language to the situation, and facilitating when needed. Bands A–F.

---

## Comparison with RSHE

RSHE specifies communication content from primary "how to communicate effectively and manage conflict with kindness and respect; how to be assertive and express needs and boundaries" [rshe-pilot-extracts §37] through to secondary "the practical steps pupils can take and skills they can develop to support respectful and kind relationships" [rshe-pilot-extracts §32]. By Band F, REAL's LT 5.1 has students calibrating how they speak — formal or informal, direct or indirect — to the listener's culture, generation, or position [real-framework-overview §51].

---

## Comparison with Welsh CfW

CfW's WM5 progression on communication runs alongside REAL's. PS3: "I can communicate my needs and feelings, and respond to those of others" [cfw-pilot-extracts §15]; PS5: "I can make meaningful connections with others, valuing safe, healthy and equitable relationships in a range of contexts" [cfw-pilot-extracts §16]. REAL's LT 5.1 follows the same A–E shape and adds Band F awareness of high/low-context cultures, hierarchy norms, and formality conventions [real-framework-overview §51].

---

## Comparison with CASEL

CASEL builds multi-context practice into the framework's structure. The five competencies are wrapped in four settings — classrooms, schools, families and caregivers, communities — making the settings part of the framework, not a backdrop [casel-framework-definition §10–§13]. Relationship Skills at G6–8 explicitly addresses "interactions on social and digital media" [casel-pilot-extracts §24]. REAL's LT 5.1 develops similar communication content at the LT level; what CASEL has and REAL doesn't is the framework-level commitment that competence shows up across nested settings, not in classrooms alone.
