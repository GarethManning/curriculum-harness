**Document:** Relationships Education, Relationships and Sex Education (RSE) and Health Education  
**Publisher:** Department for Education (DfE), England  
**Date:** July 2025  
**Statutory authority:** Children and Social Work Act 2017  
**Implementation date:** 1 September 2026  
**URL:** https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/6970e7e67e827090d02d42e0/Relationships_education_relationships_and_sex_education__RSE__and_health_education__for_intro_1_September_2026_.pdf

## Scope ingested for this corpus

This corpus covers the **full programme of study** — both primary and secondary phases — extracted from the statutory document. Four sections are included:

| Section | Pages (PDF) | Phase | Ages |
|---|---|---|---|
| Relationships education (Primary) | 8–13 | Primary | KS1+KS2 (5–11) |
| Secondary RSE curriculum content | 14–20 | Secondary | KS3+KS4 (11–16) |
| Primary health and wellbeing | 22–26 | Primary | KS1+KS2 (5–11) |
| Secondary health and wellbeing | 28–32 | Secondary | KS3+KS4 (11–16) |

**Excluded:** Implementation guidance (pp. 33–41), appendices/annexes (pp. 42–47), policy context introduction (pp. 1–7), page 21 (Health Education section header page). These are not curriculum content.

## Progression structure

The DfE RSHE guidance uses a **two-phase architecture**, not the four-KS structure of the National Curriculum:

- **End of Primary** — "content to be covered by the end of primary" (ages 5–11)
- **End of Secondary** — "content to be covered by the end of secondary" (ages 11–16)

Neither phase is subdivided into KS1/KS2 or KS3/KS4 within the curriculum content sections. Within-phase sequencing is a school decision.

## Notes on source fidelity

- This is the **DfE statutory document**, not the PSHE Association non-statutory guidance, the Welsh Curriculum for Wales RSHE, or any other variant.
- The statutory requirements under the Children and Social Work Act 2017 apply to maintained schools and academies in **England only**.
- Sex education is **not compulsory at primary**; the statutory guidance recommends (but does not require) that primaries teach sex education in Years 5–6.
- The document was updated in July 2025 following the withdrawn 2019 version; the 2025 version includes updated content on online safety, puberty, and mental health.
