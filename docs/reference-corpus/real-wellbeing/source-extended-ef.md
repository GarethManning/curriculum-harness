# REAL School Wellbeing Framework — Band E and F Extension

*Version 1 | 20 April 2026 | Extending the KUD Chart (source.md) to Bands E and F*

---

## About this document

This document extends the fourteen learning targets of the REAL School wellbeing framework from Bands A–D (documented in `source.md`) to Bands E and F. Only Band E and Band F rows are shown here; Bands A–D are unchanged and remain in `source.md`.

- **Band E**: G9–10, ages 13–15, UK Y9–Y10
- **Band F**: G11–12, ages 15–17, UK Y11–Y12

Progression across Bands E and F uses the same six progression levers applied in Bands A–D: **independence**, **complexity**, **scope**, **precision**, **reasoning**, **transfer**. Disciplinary warrants extend naturally from those in Bands A–D where the same research lineage applies; new sources are introduced where Band E/F content requires them.

Do statements for Type 3 dispositional LTs are observation indicators (third-person for LT 7.2; first-person elsewhere). Do statements for Type 2 horizontal LTs are rubric-applicable task descriptions. Format matches `source.md`.

### LT 1.1 — Self-Awareness & Regulation

**Knowledge Type:** Type 3 — Dispositional **Assessment Route:** Multi-informant observation; Reflection 360; not summatively graded **Prerequisites:** LT 6.1 (knowledge-contingent)

| Band | Do — Observation Indicator (new) | Understand (new) | Progression lever | Disciplinary warrant |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| E | I can independently sustain my regulation strategies through sustained high-pressure periods, adapt them proactively rather than reactively, and reflect on how my regulation affects others around me. | Regulation is not only a personal tool — my emotional state shapes the people around me, and taking responsibility for that is part of mature self-regulation. | Independence: prompted → proactive; Transfer: personal performance → interpersonal impact | Co-regulation and social baseline theory (Coan); advanced emotion regulation (Gross) |
| F | I can articulate my personal regulation system — the strategies I rely on, the conditions that disrupt it, and the adaptations I have built over time — and support others in developing their own. | A mature regulation system is one I can describe and teach, not merely use — and the capacity to support others' regulation deepens my own. | Independence: full self-direction; Reasoning: evaluated adjustment → system-level articulation; Transfer: self-regulation → coaching and modelling for others | Expert self-regulation (Boekaerts); peer mentoring in social-emotional learning |

## Progression notes

**Independence arc (Bands E–F):** Both bands are characterised by increasing self-direction. Band E is "independent within supported school contexts"; Band F is "fully self-directed and transferring to life beyond school." LTs where adult prompting was still expected in Band D (particularly Type 3 dispositionals) should show complete removal of prompt-dependency at Band E.

**Transfer arc:** Band E applies established skills in more complex and institutionally embedded contexts (online, legal, group, systemic). Band F explicitly targets transfer to adult life contexts, capstone projects, and community-facing contributions.

**Warrant continuity:** All Band A–D warrants extend naturally. New additions in Bands E–F:
- Allostatic load (McEwen) — LT 6.1 Band E
- Critical neuroscience (Choudhury & Slaby) — LT 6.1 Band F
- Social determinants of health (Marmot) — LT 3.1 Band E
- Systematic review methodology (Cochrane/GRADE) — LT 6.2 Band F
- Self-authorship theory (Kegan) — LT 7.1/7.2, LT 3.2, LT 5.2 Band F
- Youth leadership (Youniss) and civic education — LT 5.2 Band E
