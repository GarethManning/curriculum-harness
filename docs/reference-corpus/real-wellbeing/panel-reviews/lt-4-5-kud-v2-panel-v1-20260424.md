### Prose critique

This is a T3 dispositional KUD with third-person observation indicators rather than scoreable performance criteria, and it holds that discipline throughout. The "assessment is multi-informant observation over time; no single performance occasion is sufficient" claim is stated explicitly and is reflected in every Do cell — none of them ask the student to produce evidence on demand. That is the correct structural move for a T3 LT and reduces the assessment-of-learning contamination that this kind of disposition work is prone to.

The partial-evidence protocol reference extended across Bands D, E, and F (v2 Koedinger fix) is an inter-rater improvement: teachers can now record component-by-component evidence rather than collapse multiple components into a binary judgement. The Understand–Do independence holds at each band — Band A's Understand ("there are things I can do that help") makes a claim that is not derivable from observing acceptance of co-regulation alone; Band D's Understand (anticipatory preparation) is structurally distinct from the Do (strategies, help-seeking, repair).

Where the artefact is weaker for my lens is operational ambiguity in modifiers doing load-bearing work. "Beginning, on some occasions" at Band B is undefined — how many occasions over what window? "At least one occasion" for rupture-and-repair at Band D sets a floor that could reward a single performed attempt rather than the disposition. "Consistently applying" at Band E versus "consistently managing" at Band F shares vocabulary heavily; the differentiator is context (novel-unfamiliar vs high-stakes-accumulating) and a careful teacher will hold that distinction, but two teachers on the same student could disagree.

The KUD is also not standalone-deliverable: it points to a T3 observation protocol, a partial-evidence protocol, and an exemplar library as companion documents, which is correctly flagged but means a teacher working from the KUD alone cannot achieve the inter-rater reliability the assessment architecture requires. ## Role 2 — Dispositions Theorist

**Model:** opus
**Mean role:** 91.5
**Dimension scores:** developmental_accuracy 92, domain_grounding 91

### Prose critique

This KUD is genuinely framed as disposition, not performance, and the framing is held through to the assessment architecture — multi-informant observation across a sustained window, not an engineered performance occasion. That is the right structural commitment for T3 work, and it is rare. Most school wellbeing frameworks collapse under commercial pressure into rubric-scoreable on-demand tasks that train behavioural performance. This one does not.

The progression-lever naming — Independence, Complexity, Transfer, Scope, Reasoning, Precision — tracks deepening of stance, not escalation of vocabulary. A→B is adult-led-coregulation to self-initiation-emerging; that is a dispositional move, not a cognitive-content upgrade. C→D introduces the window-of-tolerance schema and rupture-and-repair initiation — these are stance changes, not topic changes. E→F moves from adapting strategies to having a metacognitive model of one's own pattern — the schema the student holds about themselves has deepened.

The Band A co-regulation-as-disposition decision deserves specific credit. The easy authorial move would have been to mark Band A N/A, treating self-management as beginning at Band B. That move encodes a blind spot: it treats co-regulation as a lower-bar substitute for self-regulation rather than as the developmental precursor through which self-regulation is built. The authoring correctly refuses this and holds Band A as genuine dispositional territory.

The Band F Understand v2 revision is the strongest dispositions-theory improvement. V1's "pattern I have built through repeated choices" locates disposition in deliberate trained behaviour. V2's "built through repeated occasions — some chosen, some shaped by what I have lived through" locates disposition in the fuller reality of how it actually forms, including structural and relational shaping the student did not choose. This is both more accurate to the disposition literature and more equity-defensible. The observer-calibration note on Band F component (4) — distinguishing performed reflection from model-in-use — is exactly the dispositions-theory discipline the framework needs at its upper boundary.

Where I would push harder is that the "portability" claim in the Band D note and Band F Understand is doing conceptual load-bearing work as the dispositional property that separates genuine cultivation from trained routine, but it is not named as a criterion-bank dimension in its own right. Portability across novel contexts is what makes a disposition real and not a routine; it should earn its own observation lens.

- **Minor** — "Portability" is treated as a signalled but not instrumented dispositional property; should be named as an observation dimension in criterion bank. (Location: Band D note; Band F Understand)

## Role 5 — Developmental Neuroscience Reviewer

**Model:** opus
**Mean role:** 90.0
**Dimension scores:** developmental_accuracy 88, domain_grounding 92

### Prose critique

Trauma-informed, window-of-tolerance lens. The Band A authoring decision is correct: a K-2 child's regulatory capacity is scaffolded-by-adult, and the framework recognises this by observing co-regulation-acceptance and strategy-attempt rather than demanding autonomous regulation the nervous system cannot yet produce. The Zeman 2006 citation is appropriate and the Siegel citations at Bands C and D are the right sources for the claims being made.

The Band F Understand v2 revision ("built through repeated occasions — some chosen, some shaped by what I have lived through") is the single most important trauma-informed change in this KUD. V1's "pattern I have built through repeated choices" would have pathologised students whose patterns were shaped by abuse, neglect, or structural adversity — a student who learned emotional shutdown because expression was unsafe did not "choose" that pattern, and asking them to relate to their pattern as self-authored would have been iatrogenic. V2 acknowledges that patterns form through occasions beyond the student's control while preserving the generative insight (patterns are changeable if sustaining conditions are understood). This is correct practice. The Band A Do physical-comfort softening is also safety-essential: children with trauma histories may find physical contact triggering even when offered supportively, and the v2 wording accommodates safeguarding boundaries.

Where this KUD carries unresolved safeguarding risk: Band F component (3), "recovering from significant emotional setbacks in ways that allow re-engagement with their commitments," treats severe-adverse-event responses as a single observable category. At G11-12, "significant emotional setbacks" can include bereavement, disclosure of abuse, onset of eating-disorder symptoms, first trauma response, or suicidal ideation. The appropriate response to many of these is clinical referral, not continued observation against the framework. The KUD does not specify an escalation path; it assumes the form tutor or subject teacher who observes is equipped to discriminate what-to-observe from what-to-refer.

Band D rupture-and-repair carries a parallel risk: "proposing a forward step" in a relational rupture where the other party was the aggressor (bullying, racial harm, sexualised harm) asks the harmed student to initiate repair with someone who harmed them. The Do-cell does not distinguish peer conflict (both parties involved) from asymmetric harm. In asymmetric-harm cases the survivor should not be expected to initiate repair. This needs qualification.

Band E's "significantly impair subsequent functioning" after setback can be normative grief, which is not dysfunction; treating it as an observation of inadequate recovery would pathologise normal grieving.

- **Major** — Band F component 3 treats severe-adverse-event responses as one observable category; no safeguarding escalation path specified. (Location: Band F Do component 3)
- **Moderate** — Band D rupture-and-repair does not distinguish peer conflict from asymmetric harm; survivor should not be asked to initiate repair with harm-doer. (Location: Band D Do component 3)
- **Moderate** — Band E "significantly impair functioning" after setback can pathologise normative grief if observer cannot discriminate. (Location: Band E Do component 2)

## Role 6 — Therapeutic-Education Sceptic

**Model:** opus
**Mean role:** 71.67
**Dimension scores:** developmental_accuracy 75, domain_grounding 72, practical_usability 68

### Prose critique

The case against: this artefact operationalises an assumption that is not self-evident — that emotional self-management across K-12 is a developmental target schools should be producing longitudinal observational evidence of, across six bands, as part of a framework. The v2 revisions are real improvements (the Wineburg agency-framing fix is substantial; Damour physical-contact softening is safety-necessary; Koedinger partial-evidence extension tightens the inter-rater story), and I credit them. They do not address the underlying concerns.

First — medicalisation of ordinary difficulty. Band A Do observes whether a five-year-old "accepts a trusted adult's co-regulation support" when experiencing "anger, fear, distress, or overwhelm." Most five-year-olds are upset frequently. The act of making this a framework-recorded longitudinal observation pathologises developmentally-normal emotional reactivity by inserting it into an assessment instrument. Children who refuse adult support — also developmentally-normal and often appropriate self-protective behaviour — get recorded as not-yet-Band-A.

Second — expanded school remit. Band F Do observes "out-of-school when triangulated" emotional self-management. Schools are asking for triangulated evidence of how a seventeen-year-old regulates in family and private contexts. The consent architecture for this is not discussed.

Third — teacher as quasi-therapist. The KUD asks classroom teachers to observe, across sustained windows, patterns of rupture-and-repair, recovery from significant setbacks, and metacognitive pattern-articulation. This is therapeutic observation language. Teachers are not trained or supervised to make these observations reliably, and the framework does not specify clinical-vs-educational scope boundaries.

Fourth — individualised resilience. Even with the v2 Band F Understand revision, the Do cell locates the pattern within the student and asks them to articulate it as a working model they use. Structural conditions — family income, school resourcing, racism, homophobia, exposure to violence — are not observed as sustaining conditions. The student holds the pattern; the student leverages understanding of it; the environment that shaped it is invisible to the observation protocol.

Fifth — performance theatre. The observer-calibration note on Band F component 4 correctly identifies that articulate self-description can be performed rather than substantive. Good. But this risk applies to every band: children learn which behaviours the framework wants to see. The more the framework is used, the more students learn to perform compliance with it.

Sixth — what the framework cannot see. Children who suppress emotional expression because they have learned it is unsafe produce data that reads as Band A success (accepting co-regulation, using strategies) but is in fact a safety pattern. The framework has no way to distinguish a well-regulated child from a frozen child. This is the deepest problem and it is not acknowledged.

My scores reflect within-frame quality against a frame I remain sceptical of: the content is developmentally-matched to the frame it operates in; the citations are real but their synthesis into a school-assessment instrument is a translation the source literature does not directly authorise; the deliverability depends on clinical-adjacent judgements classroom teachers are not trained to make.

### Flags

- **Major** — Band F out-of-school triangulation has no consent architecture discussed. (Location: Band F Do)
- **Major** — Classroom teachers asked to make clinical-adjacent observations without scope boundaries or escalation architecture. (Location: Whole KUD; especially Bands E, F)
- **Major** — Structural and environmental sustaining conditions of emotional pattern are invisible to observation protocol; individualises resilience. (Location: Band F Understand; Do components)
- **Major** — Framework cannot distinguish well-regulated child from frozen child producing compliance-appearing data. (Location: Bands A–C Do cells)
- **Moderate** — Risk that cumulative framework use teaches students to perform framework-compliant behaviour rather than develop disposition. (Location: Whole KUD)
- **Moderate** — Source literature (emotion-regulation primary research) does not directly authorise synthesis into a school K-12 progression framework. (Location: Disciplinary warrants across bands)

The non-theatre quality of the FAIL is that I can identify specific things that, if fixed, would lift the mean above 88: (1) specifying the safeguarding escalation path at Bands E–F explicitly in the KUD (not deferred to protocol documents) would move developmental_neuroscience_reviewer from 88 to perhaps 91, and therapeutic_education_sceptic from 75 to perhaps 78 on developmental_accuracy; (2) specifying a minimum LT 4.4 schema requirement at Bands D–F Know rather than allowing proceed-anyway would move curriculum_specificity_reviewer specificity from 84 to perhaps 87 and cognitive_load_architect specificity from 85 to perhaps 88; (3) pulling the Band F component 4 observer-calibration discipline from Authoring Notes into the Do cell itself would address a unanimous flag across four roles and move assessment_architect structural_integrity from 89 to perhaps 91. If all three were done, mean_overall would plausibly land around 86.5–87.5 — still below 88, but closer. The distance remaining could be closed by structural conditions observation protocols (addressing the sceptic's fourth concern), at which point the mean would cross 88.

So the FAIL is defensible: this artefact has real remaining work, and the panel identifies what it is. The FAIL is not theatre in the sense of being arbitrary or ritualistic. But the FAIL also signals that the 88 threshold, as currently set, treats the work as not-publication-ready when the author considers it high-quality — which is precisely the empirical question Phase 0.5 exists to answer.

### Tuning recommendations for Phase 0.5

**Recommendation 1 — Threshold calibration.** Phase 0.5 should run the panel-review skill against both LT 4.5 KUD v2 (this artefact — Gareth-considered-high-quality) and a known-flawed comparator (e.g. LT 4.4 KUD v1 or a constructed flawed version with seeded issues) and examine the separation between them. If LT 4.5 KUD v2 lands at 85.07 and the flawed comparator lands at 72.0, the threshold has discriminatory power and could plausibly be recalibrated from 88 to 82 or 83 (or lower) with evidence recorded. If both artefacts land in the low-80s, the skill is under-discriminating and role briefs need tightening. If LT 4.5 KUD v2 lands in the high-80s when re-run, reviewer-run variance is high and the skill needs either multiple runs averaged or tighter prompt constraints. The priority Phase 0.5 question is not whether 88 is right — it is whether the skill produces a reliable monotonic signal from artefact-quality-as-judged-by-Gareth to skill-output.

**Recommendation 2 — Role briefs are distinguishability-strong; do not retune for distinguishability before Phase 0.5.** The distinguishability analysis above shows all seven roles producing strong vocabulary, characteristic-concerns, and voice markers. No role produced generic curriculum-review output. Role briefs do not need tightening on distinguishability grounds. Any brief changes before Phase 0.5 should be limited to clarifying the scoring convention when a dimension is compromised by a frame the reviewer opposes (see Recommendation 3).

**Recommendation 3 — Clarify sceptic scoring convention in the brief.** The sceptic brief says "If your score for a dimension is 45 because the dimension itself is compromised by a therapeutic-education frame, say so." That permission is important but creates real ambiguity in practice: does the sceptic score the artefact *within the frame it operates in* (in which case this KUD is defensibly matched to its T3 dispositional frame, and dimension scores would sit in the low-to-mid 80s), or does the sceptic score *whether the frame itself is appropriate* (in which case scores drop into the 60s–70s regardless of within-frame quality). The worked example (sceptic at 66.67) and my run (sceptic at 71.67) both operate in the hybrid space between these conventions. Phase 0.5 could benefit from a sharper specification: e.g. "Sceptic scores within-frame quality on the first-and-third dimensions (developmental accuracy, domain grounding, practical usability where applicable); sceptic scores the frame's appropriateness through the flags list and the critique prose, not through the dimension scores." This would raise sceptic dimension scores into the 75–85 range and move the mean_overall upward, tightening alignment between "artefact is high quality" and "panel verdict is PASS" without losing the sceptic's structural dissent role (which would continue to surface through major flags even when dimension scores are higher). Alternatively, the brief could keep the current convention and raise the floor from 70 to 75, which would make the sceptic's floor role more protective without inflating the mean.

**Recommendation 4 — Dimension coverage balance.** Practical_usability is scored by only three roles (assessment_architect, relational_sel_practitioner, therapeutic_education_sceptic), and one of those three (the sceptic) has an adversarial stance on the dimension as a matter of brief. This makes practical_usability uniquely sceptic-sensitive: one sceptic score drops the dimension mean by ~8 points (as happened here, 85.5 → 79.67). Phase 0.5 could consider adding cognitive_load_architect to practical_usability or replacing one of the current three roles to dilute sceptic weight on this single dimension. Without change, a sceptic score of 68 on practical_usability will reliably drag mean_overall by 1.5–2 points across all artefact runs, which may systematically disadvantage T3 dispositional work relative to T2 content (the sceptic has less traction on T2).

**Recommendation 5 — Do not introduce new roles before Phase 0.5 completes.** The seven-role panel is producing distinguishable, substantive output on a real artefact; adding roles before Phase 0.5 threshold validation would confound the calibration study with role-set changes. Hold the seven-role set; validate against known-high-quality and known-flawed artefacts; tune threshold and floor after; consider role additions (Koedinger-equivalent for KC depth; a literacies reviewer for cross-discipline KUDs) in a later phase only.
