# Circle Solutions SEL Framework
## Cowie, H. & Myers, C-A. (2016)

Source: Cowie, H. & Myers, C-A. — Circle Solutions for Student Wellbeing
Tables 2.1 and 2.2: Twelve dimensions for learning relationships and promoting wellbeing
Captured: 21 April 2026

Note: This framework maps against CASEL competencies but is a distinct framework 
with its own 12-dimension structure. It includes an assessment framework with 
observable indicators at Year 2, Year 6, Year 9, and Year 12, plus a 1-5 
achievement scale per dimension.

---

## Twelve SEL Dimensions (Table 2.1)

### Dimension 1: Self-awareness — Being and becoming
**Content:** Identification of values, beliefs, strengths and goals.
**Context:** Clarity of school values, vision, priorities and direction. A focus on the wellbeing of the whole child.

### Dimension 2: Emotional awareness and knowledge — The biology and psychology of emotion; personal triggers; social construction
**Content:** Understanding the range of emotions and how they are experienced within the body. Awareness of personal, social and cultural influences on feelings.
**Context:** Emotional climate of the school and how this is demonstrated. Awareness of influences on this development.

### Dimension 3: Emotional skills — Regulation; expression; coping and resilience
**Content:** Dealing with and regulating negative emotion. Acceptable expression of feelings. Knowing what sustains emotional wellbeing and promotes resilience.
**Context:** Awareness of adult models of emotional literacy. Communication of expectations — validating feelings but not accepting hurtful words and actions. Staff wellbeing.

### Dimension 4: Shared humanity — How do we position others in our world?
**Content:** Appreciating uniqueness for the self and others. Valuing diversity while seeking what is shared. Inclusive belonging.
**Context:** Celebration of diversity. Actively addressing racism, sexism and homophobia. Inclusive policies for students with special needs.

### Dimension 5: Interpersonal skills — Skills needed to establish and maintain positive relationships with others
**Content:** Exploring the meaning and practice of relational values. Positive communication skills. Collaboration and cooperation.
**Context:** Positive teacher-student relationships. Student and staff voice. Staff collegiality. Collaborative pedagogies. Positive communication practices. Support systems.

### Dimension 6: Situational skills — Tuning into the emotional context
**Content:** Empathy. Reading, interpreting and tuning into emotions in situations. Not pre-judging. Awareness of timing.
**Context:** Taking account of emotions in situations of challenge, change, failure and loss. Flexibility and appropriate responsiveness in highly charged situations.

### Dimension 7: Leadership — Goal setting; personal confidence; empowering others
**Content:** Goal setting. Taking initiative and responsibility. Confidence. Dealing with peer pressure. Empowering others.
**Context:** Communication of vision. Acknowledging and trusting others. Genuine consultation. 'Walking the talk'. Avoiding blame. Being in charge, but not controlling others.

### Dimension 8: Promoting the positive — Strengths and solutions approach
**Content:** Optimism. Gratitude. Humour. Perspective. Identification of strengths in self and others.
**Context:** Identifying what is going well and ways to increase this. Working with the positive. Positive behaviour policies. Pedagogies for fun, meaning and engagement.

### Dimension 9: Conflict and confrontation — Dealing well with relational difficulties
**Content:** Negotiation. Compromise. Appropriate assertiveness. Problem-solving skills.
**Context:** Pre-empting potential conflict. Appropriate use of authority. De-escalating confrontation. Addressing conflict actively. Anti-bullying policies.

### Dimension 10: Repair and restoration — Mending damage in relationships and restoring community
**Content:** Acknowledging hurt. 'Doing' sorry. Willingness to compromise. Responding to repair overtures. Action to repair harm.
**Context:** Restorative approaches to behaviour. Mistakes as part of learning for all. Adults willing to acknowledge error. Policies of reintegration for excluded students.

### Dimension 11: Human rights dimensions of SEL — Ethics and integrity
**Content:** Honesty, trust. Consistency. Ethical decision-making. Focus on human rights.
**Context:** Core values in social justice. Congruence between values, policies and practices. Authenticity.

### Dimension 12: Spirituality — Seeking meaning in life
**Content:** Having a secure identity. Developing a philosophy for life. Environmental responsibility.
**Context:** Educational philosophy focused on the whole child in all dimensions of development alongside community/global awareness and contribution to others.

## Assessment Framework — Observable Indicators by Year Level (Table 2.2)

Assessment uses a 1-5 scale:
- 5 Achievement plus
- 4 Achievement
- 3 Working towards
- 2 Made a start
- 1 Not there yet

---

### Dimension 1: Self-awareness

**Year 2:**
- A student can draw a picture of themselves and describe what they have drawn. 'This is me, my name is and I am...' Students are asked to say as much as they can.
- Can say five things that they like about themselves.
- Can say three things that are important to them.

**Year 6:**
- A student can draw three pictures of themselves in different roles and can talk about the different ways they behave in each of these roles. This is me at home. This is me at school. This is me with my friends. Here I am...
- Can identify three things they are good at, three qualities they have and three strengths they think they might need in the future.
- Can say three things that are important to them and why they are important.

**Year 9:**
- Has reflected on their developing identity. Writes about a role model and why they have chosen this person.
- Can talk about how they have developed up to five character strengths and what strengths they are working on and why these are valuable.
- Can identify the five values and three beliefs about the world that impact most in how they choose to live their lives.

**Year 12:**
- 'Me in ten years' time.' Can write a page about themselves, who they are, what they believe and the person they hope to become in the future.
- Can identify five character strengths and also the shadow sides of strengths. Can say what strengths have been of most value to them in their life.
- Are clear about a range of values and the reasons they have these.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: has high level of self-awareness and is clear about their identity at an age-appropriate level.
4 — Achievement: can identify strengths and values both in theory and in practice.
3 — Working towards: knows strengths and values but puts them into practice only intermittently.
2 — Made a start: finds it hard to identify positive qualities or what is important to them.
1 — Not there yet: poor or negative self-image in most contexts.

---

### Dimension 2: Emotional knowledge

**Year 2:**
- Can talk about what happens in their bodies when they feel happy, angry and scared.
- Listens to different pieces of music and can say what it makes them feel.

**Year 6:**
- Knows about the continuum of emotions. Can take part in a discussion about when you might be in control of an emotion and when it controls you and say what makes a difference.
- Can take part in a discussion about how the arts (music/dance and drama) can impact on how someone feels.

**Year 9:**
- Knows the role of the amygdala and how it can hijack 'thinking'. Can give an example of when this might happen. Knows about 'mirroring' neurons. Can give an example of when this might happen.
- Watches a film and writes a review about the characters, what happened to them and the emotional content. What did it make them feel and why?

**Year 12:**
- Can tell the difference between being sad and being depressed. Knows who to seek out for support when they are struggling.
- Can write a page about an historical event in which people's emotions were manipulated and how this happened.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: has emotional knowledge beyond what could be expected for their age.
4 — Achievement: has an age-appropriate level of emotional knowledge, can complete the tasks given and generalise in other contexts.
3 — Working towards: has some emotional knowledge but does not often put knowledge into practice.
2 — Made a start: on knowing about feelings but understanding is inconsistent.
1 — Not there yet: very basic awareness of feelings, how they are experienced in the body and what influences them.

---

### Dimension 3: Emotional skills — regulation, expression and resilience

**Year 2:**
- Uses regular breathing as a calming strategy.
- Uses at least twelve different words for emotion in describing: a play situation; losing something; a day at home.
- Can say what cheers them up when they are fed up.

**Year 6:**
- Can identify four different ways to deal with anger and decide which is the preferred option and why.
- Can identify at least five words showing different aspects of the following emotions: happiness; sadness; interest; anger.
- Knows how thinking can affect how you feel and says what thinking strategies are useful.

**Year 9:**
- Can list five ways in which someone might change how they feel and write the pros and cons of each.
- Can reflect on a learning task and describe a range of emotions that were experienced in engagement, challenges and completion.
- Can identify ways in which friends can support resilience and ways in which this can be undermined.

**Year 12:**
- Is using one physical strategy, one social strategy and one cognitive strategy on a regular basis to help stay resilient.
- Joins in a discussion about a current controversial issue or a film, talking about how people might feel about this.
- Has considered the usefulness of mindfulness or meditation as a resilience strategy.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: has an extensive vocabulary for emotion for age and uses a wide range of strategies to regulate emotions and stay resilient.
4 — Achievement: can complete the tasks given and is using the skills across contexts.
3 — Working towards: can talk about feelings but vocabulary is limited; occasionally uses calming strategies without prompts.
2 — Made a start: has a basic language of feelings, attempts to regulate emotions with prompts.
1 — Not there yet: expresses feelings without words and is not yet responsive to prompts to use calming strategies.

---

### Dimension 4: Shared humanity — valuing uniqueness and diversity

**Year 2:**
- Can say three things that are the same and three things that are different about themselves and another person.
- Joins in a discussion about how this class can make sure everyone feels they belong here.

**Year 6:**
- Can write about ways in which everyone is unique and the reasons it is valuable.
- Can say what 'The Golden Rule' is, how it appears in all major religions and what it means in practice.

**Year 9:**
- Can give five reasons why 'shared humanity' matters for the future of our world.
- With a partner makes a poster on gender, sexuality or disability to prevent discrimination.

**Year 12:**
- Can identify times where people have been dehumanized, what contributes to this and what has happened to them.
- Is able to identify ways in which shared humanity might be put into practice from the individual to the community to the social/political level.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: understands the concept at an age-appropriate level and takes positive action on including everyone.
4 — Achievement: demonstrates acceptance of everyone.
3 — Working towards: can talk about the importance of each individual.
2 — Made a start: goes along with others' views.
1 — Not there yet: comments negatively on difference.

---

### Dimension 5: Interpersonal skills — positive communication, relational values, collaboration and friendship

**Year 2:**
- Can say 'personal positives' about classmates.
- Demonstrates the ability to share.
- In a pair share, can identify three qualities or actions that would make someone a good friend.

**Year 6:**
- Demonstrates the 'rules' of good conversation — a balance between talking and listening.
- Demonstrates regular small acts of kindness — goes out of their way to help others.
- Works well within a group to achieve a given target.

**Year 9:**
- Demonstrates the skills of 'active listening', paying verbal and non-verbal attention, responding verbally and non-verbally, asking questions, clarifying meaning.
- Knows what respect means, how it is demonstrated and why it is important for a healthy relationship.
- Can initiate and maintain a conversation with someone who is previously unknown.

**Year 12:**
- Is effective within a group discussion. Includes others and builds on their ideas.
- Knows what trust means, how it is demonstrated and why it is important for a healthy relationship.
- Works with a group to take a project from initiation to completion and can write about the factors that promoted collaboration between team members.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: demonstrates a wide range of relational values at an age-appropriate level and is an effective communicator and collaborator across contexts.
4 — Achievement: is usually friendly towards others.
3 — Working towards: takes part in Circle activities and is beginning to generalise skills.
2 — Made a start: takes part in Circle activities but not yet generalizing learning.
1 — Not there yet: is not interested in others and is either reluctant to engage or engages negatively.

---

### Dimension 6: Situational skills — empathy, awareness of others, timing

**Year 2:**
- Can say how a teddy might feel if he: was left out; saw someone fall off a bike; had a new baby sister.
- Can recognize non-verbal expression of basic emotions — happy, surprised, scared, sad and worried.

**Year 6:**
- Shows an understanding of what someone might feel if they: were bullied; lost something or someone; broke a leg; failed a test; were hungry or tired.
- Can view a film and identify the feelings of characters within a situation by their voice tone, body posture and facial expression.

**Year 9:**
- Can say when it might be a good time to approach a parent with a request and when best to wait, and identify factors that influence the decision.
- Recognizes that everyone has their own story and does not make quick judgements.

**Year 12:**
- Can assess the emotional content of a situation and say what might be said or done to make this worse or better. This can be from a book or film.
- Can write a story from the different perspectives of the main characters.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: demonstrates exceptional sensitivity to others and does not make judgements on limited information.
4 — Achievement: is able to tune into what others might be feeling in a given situation and is aware of what might make something worse or better.
3 — Working towards: understands what someone might be feeling in a given situation.
2 — Made a start: shows some caring towards others.
1 — Not there yet: has limited awareness of what others are feeling.

---

### Dimension 7: Leadership — goal setting, personal confidence, empowering others

**Year 2:**
- Can say three things they want to achieve this week.
- Invites others to join in games.
- Is willing to try new things.

**Year 6:**
- Demonstrates an interest in getting better at something. Understands the concept of personal bests.
- Is aware of those who have been left out and includes them.
- Accepts mistakes as part of learning.

**Year 9:**
- Can divide a task into a series of between 8 and 12 achievable steps.
- Can discuss risk taking and look at how to assess risk against potential outcomes.
- Gives help to others so that they can do well at something.

**Year 12:**
- Can reflect on a personal achievement and what contributed to success.
- Can say what helps resist peer pressure so you act according to your own values.
- Can identify plans for the future, with fall-back plans if these don't happen.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: has authentic confidence in many areas: encourages others to participate and shine, sets goals and plans how to get there.
4 — Achievement: demonstrates two out of the three above.
3 — Working towards: demonstrates one out of the three above.
2 — Made a start: offers to help others sometimes; recovers quickly from making a mistake.
1 — Not there yet: highly anxious about performance and/or absorbed with being 'better' than others.

---

### Dimension 8: Promoting the positive — solution focus, thankfulness, optimism

**Year 2:**
- In a paired discussion, identifies a situation where someone is laughing at someone and how this might feel, and when someone is laughing with someone and how this might feel.
- Engages with a class project on making others feel good (filling buckets).

**Year 6:**
- Identifies three things to be grateful for and three things to look forward to.
- Can identify one situation in which positive thinking helped and can talk/write about the difference it made.

**Year 9:**
- Keeps a daily gratitude journal for a month: writing in three different blessings, one for the day, one for life and one for anything else.
- Can take five 'problems' and give a 'solution' for each: what you are aiming for rather than what you want to get rid of.

**Year 12:**
- Reflects on the future, and makes a video talking about what they hope for themselves and the world in the future. Writes three things that they will do now to help this happen.
- Talks/writes about a life event as if it were a newspaper story, once with the headline 'Disaster' and once with the headline 'Success', identifying how things can be seen from different perspectives and we have some choice.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: uses positive thinking and positive approaches in nearly all situations.
4 — Achievement: often engages with positive approaches.
3 — Working towards: can sometimes be optimistic, thankful and/or solution focused.
2 — Made a start: is aware there are choices in the way you think about something.
1 — Not there yet: is almost entirely focused on problems and how awful things are.

---

### Dimension 9: Dealing with conflict — appropriate assertiveness, negotiation, problem-solving skills

**Year 2:**
- Clearly, firmly and politely states what they want — e.g. please leave me alone now.
- Knows when something is fair or not and how to stand up for someone.

**Year 6:**
- Stands up for others who are less able to stand up for themselves.
- Knows when a situation is not safe and leaves.

**Year 9:**
- Identifies the short- and long-term outcomes of overt aggression, passive aggression, timidity and appropriate assertiveness.
- Checks on intention before jumping to a negative conclusion in a potential conflict.

**Year 12:**
- With a real-life or imagined scenario, can imagine three possible resolutions and write the pros and cons of each, and give a rationale for the first choice.
- Checks out what two sides want in a situation and negotiates a compromise.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: can manage most conflict situations with skill and diplomacy.
4 — Achievement: behaves with appropriate assertiveness in most situations.
3 — Working towards: knows how to handle conflict but skills not always put into practice.
2 — Made a start: occasionally shows how to handle a difficult situation well.
1 — Not there yet: is either aggressive or passive in most conflict situations.

---

### Dimension 10: Repair and restoration — acknowledging hurt, offering and responding to repair offers

**Year 2:**
- Checks whether a hurt was an accident or deliberate.
- Can talk about things that are 'inside' hurts and those that are 'outside' hurts.

**Year 6:**
- Knows about responsibility pie-charts: how much was down to me, how much to someone else, how much to chance.
- Offers to make up for harm caused.

**Year 9:**
- Can list ways to say how something could have been done differently.
- Responds positively to an attempt to repair harm caused.

**Year 12:**
- Joins in a discussion about the value of truth and reconciliation trials in war-torn countries.
- Composes a letter asking for forgiveness for a past deed (this does not have to be sent).

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: is quick to take responsibility when appropriate and offers to 'do sorry' in negotiation with others concerned, without prompting.
4 — Achievement: acknowledges responsibility for actions, shows remorse and will engage in a restorative conference. Responds positively to repair overtures.
3 — Working towards: is able to reflect on responsibility and acknowledge what might have been done differently. Is usually willing to engage in restorative conferencing.
2 — Made a start: says sorry and offers restoration when prompted/encouraged.
1 — Not there yet: does not yet take responsibility for causing harm and usually blames others.

---

### Dimension 11: Ethics, integrity and human rights — honesty, trust and social justice, global and environmental responsibility

**Year 2:**
- Can say how a lie can grow and get out of control and give three reasons why it might be better to tell the truth.
- Within a small group, identifies the responsibilities that go with five rights or freedoms.

**Year 6:**
- In a small group, talks about why people don't always get what they deserve, and what this means for how we see the world and others in it.
- In small groups, using the Strengths in Circles cards on inclusion and equity, chooses one from each set and decides how this will be put into practice in the classroom.

**Year 9:**
- Identifies three factors that influence how someone might vote in an election.
- Takes part in a project to protect the environment.

**Year 12:**
- Joins in a small group discussion on the meaning of altruism and whether this is a value to aim for.
- Within a small group, researches a social justice issue and devises an action plan to redress this.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: reflects on human rights and takes at least one small action to promote these.
4 — Achievement: demonstrates an understanding of responsibility to others.
3 — Working towards: shows concern about others and/or the environment.
2 — Made a start: understands that fairness matters for everyone.
1 — Not there yet: has a limited view of the world to protect the self.

---

### Dimension 12: Meaning and spirituality — having a clear identity and developing a philosophy for life

**Year 2:**
- Grows a flower from a seed. Talks in a pair share about what happened: Did the flower grow by itself? What helped it grow? How much was nature and how much was what you did to help?
- Identifies three things that would make someone happy for a short time and three things that would make someone happy all or most of the time.

**Year 6:**
- Chooses a symbol or picture that illustrates what really matters in life and talks about the choice.
- Identifies someone who is a role model for how to live life well and researches what that person did.

**Year 9:**
- Joins in a discussion about what it means to live 'a good life'.
- Makes a photo portfolio of what is meaningful and writes briefly why each photo is included.

**Year 12:**
- Writes or speaks about the principles or values that will guide small and major decisions in the future.
- Writes a personal statement setting out aspirations for being, becoming and contributing.

**Summary scale:**
5 — Achievement plus: has a clear sense of identity and a philosophy for life, and lives according to values and beliefs.
4 — Achievement: can talk about what is meaningful and acts on beliefs on occasions.
3 — Working towards: is experimenting with and reflecting on different ways of being and thinking.
2 — Made a start: knows what is more or less important in life.
1 — Not there yet: is swayed by what others think.
