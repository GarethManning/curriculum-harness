## 4. Sequencing Differences

**Emotional regulation and self-awareness.** All three frameworks sequence emotion regulation from simple to complex, but with different grain and pacing. REAL has a 6-band explicit progression from naming one feeling (Band A) to articulating a personal regulation system and teaching it to others (Band F). Welsh CfW has a parallel 5-step progression, tracking closely with REAL: PS1 (awareness/communication) → PS2 (understanding change) → PS3 (self-regulation with strategies) → PS4 (independent self-awareness) → PS5 (complexity of emotions, sustained orientation). The two frameworks are closely sequenced and mutually reinforcing. UK RSHE has no fine-grained regulation progression — regulation content appears only in aggregate at End of Primary ("skills for managing difficult feelings") and End of Secondary ("self-esteem, independence and having a positive relationship with oneself"), without the band-by-band development REAL and CfW both carry.

**Consent and body autonomy.** REAL introduces body ownership and trusted adults at Band A (LT 4.1), moves to boundary-setting and consent skills at Band B, generalises consent online at Band C, addresses power and peer pressure at Band D, applies legal consent knowledge at Band E, and reaches peer-education capacity at Band F. UK RSHE introduces body rights and boundaries at End of Primary (A–C) and legal/ethical consent at End of Secondary (C–E) — a 2-phase approach rather than a 6-band one. Notably, RSHE places grooming awareness firmly in the primary phase (A–C), while REAL's explicit treatment of grooming and exploitation risk appears at Band E (LT 4.1). This represents a genuine sequencing difference: RSHE treats grooming as a safeguarding topic for primary-aged children; REAL treats it as a consent-literacy topic for secondary-aged learners. A PLC decision is needed here: should REAL surface grooming awareness earlier, even if the full analytic treatment comes at Band E?

**Decision-making.** REAL's 6-band decision-making progression (LT 2.2) moves from single-observation reflection (Band A) to values-anchored choice (Band C) to bias identification and epistemic humility (Band E) to reasoned positions under irreducible uncertainty (Band F). Welsh CfW's WM3 runs a parallel progression: simple decisions (PS1) → recognising impact on others (PS2) → considered decisions with past evidence (PS3) → evidence research and goal-setting (PS4) → critical evaluation of collective decisions (PS5). These two frameworks sequence decision-making similarly, with CfW slightly more collective-minded (group decisions appear at PS1 and PS2) and REAL more individually epistemological (bias and reasoning quality become explicit from Band D). RSHE does not have an equivalent decision-making progression strand; its references to decision-making are embedded in relationships and online safety content.

**Help-seeking and mental health support.** REAL introduces the concept of a named trusted adult at Band A (LT 3.2) and builds to accessing confidential health services at Band E. Welsh CfW also introduces help-seeking early (PS1: "I am beginning to know how to respond and get help"), but the explicit mental-health framing of help-seeking — communicating feelings as a strategy for wellbeing — appears at PS3 (Bands C–D). This creates a 2-band gap in how CfW sequences mental-health-specific help-seeking relative to REAL's earliest engagement with trusted adults. UK RSHE's "Where to get advice e.g. family, school and/or other sources" appears at End of Primary (A–C), aligning with REAL's Band A introduction. None of the three frameworks sequences help-seeking identically; the PLC should consider which sequencing best serves REAL's student population.

**Physical health habits.** REAL's LT 3.1 integrates sleep, food, and movement into a unified "health habits" strand from Band B, escalating to structural analysis of health at Band E and sustainable adult health at Band F. Welsh CfW WM1 sequences physical health with significantly more granularity on nutrition (balanced diet → meal planning → meal preparation) and physical movement (motor skills → sport → independent adaptation), but does not explicitly address the habit-formation mechanisms REAL emphasises. RSHE does not have an equivalent health habits strand. This means REAL's habits-as-system framing (including habit loops, which appear in LT 6.1) is distinctive; CfW's nutrition-and-movement granularity is also distinctive. A wellbeing team might ask whether these are complementary or whether REAL should develop more specific content in the domains CfW covers.

## 5. Questions for PLC

1. **Grooming and exploitation timing.** RSHE treats grooming awareness as a primary-phase safeguarding topic (End of Primary, Bands A–C), while REAL's explicit treatment of "coercion, grooming, or exploitation risk" appears at Band E (LT 4.1). Should REAL introduce a lighter-touch, age-appropriate version of this content at Bands B or C? What would a Band B version of grooming-awareness look like in REAL's dispositional voice, and is there a risk that deferring it to Band E leaves a gap in safeguarding?

2. **Online image-sharing law.** UK RSHE dedicates multiple items at End of Secondary (Bands C–E) to the criminal law around indecent images, sextortion, and AI-generated imagery. REAL does not address these as distinct curricular topics. Given that REAL students are of secondary age (Bands C–F), does the team believe this legal knowledge is adequately addressed through REAL's consent strand (LT 4.1) or through pastoral/tutor systems — or is a gap in REAL's coverage visible?

3. **Apparent convergence on consent.** The crosswalk classifies the REAL Band D consent item as "apparent-only convergent" with RSHE's End of Secondary power-dynamics content. REAL frames consent as something to act on (refusal, exit, peer support); RSHE frames it as something to understand (knowledge of power inequalities). Does the team believe REAL's dispositional framing is sufficient for students at Band D, or should REAL explicitly incorporate propositional knowledge about the legal and power dimensions of consent at this band?

4. **Neuroscience as distinctive content.** LT 6.1 (Brain, Body & Wellbeing Science) has no parallel in either RSHE or Welsh CfW. It is the most distinctive element of the REAL framework in this crosswalk. Is this distinctiveness intentional and worth protecting — a claim about what a REAL wellbeing education uniquely offers — or does its absence from statutory and national frameworks signal a question about its core status in the framework?

5. **Metacognition and its placement.** LT 7.1 and LT 7.2 (Metacognitive Self-Direction) also have no parallel in either external framework. They are the most pedagogically unusual feature of the REAL framework at secondary level. The PLC should ask: is metacognitive self-direction a wellbeing competency (as REAL frames it), a learning-skills competency (as it would typically be framed in education research), or both? How does REAL currently assess whether students are developing along LT 7.1 and 7.2, and does the current articulation of these LTs clearly distinguish them from reflective practice more broadly?

6. **Physical movement and sport.** Welsh CfW has a full progression strand on physical movement and sport (WM1) that has no parallel in REAL. If REAL's Competency 3 (Physical Wellbeing & Self-Care) is intended to address physical health, should it include a movement or sport element? Or does REAL's school structure mean physical education is handled separately, making this gap intentional? If intentional, is it worth naming that explicitly in the framework documentation?

7. **Social influence and identity formation.** Welsh CfW's WM4 ("How we engage with social influences shapes who we are") runs an explicit 5-step progression on how groups shape identity, with students eventually advocating for positive values and challenging harmful ones. REAL addresses this indirectly through LT 2.2 (values in decisions) and LT 5.2 (community engagement) but does not name the social shaping of identity as a standalone curricular object. Is this a gap in REAL's coverage, particularly at Bands D–E where peer influence is highest, or is the team confident that LT 2.2 and LT 5.2 cover this adequately?

8. **Newly authored Band E/F content.** Twenty-eight items in REAL Bands E and F were authored in April 2026 and have not yet been reviewed by REAL teachers. Several of these appear in the convergence table with high-confidence matches against CfW PS5 content, which provides some external validity. However, the PLC should review the E/F items specifically flagged as teacher_review_flag in the source (LT 3.2/4.1/4.2/5.2 Band D — not E/F) as well as all 28 Band E/F items to confirm the disciplinary warrants and Understand statements are appropriate for REAL's pedagogical context. Are there Band E/F items in the convergence table above that surprise teachers, or items missing from the tables that teachers would expect to see?

9. **Depth of convergence vs breadth.** Several convergent rows in Section 1 are marked "apparent-only" because REAL's dispositional framing and RSHE's propositional framing describe the same topic differently. The PLC should consider: where the crosswalk shows apparent-only convergence, does REAL's version equip students with everything they need — including the factual/legal knowledge RSHE provides — or does REAL's dispositional framing leave students without knowledge they need in order to act well? Webb's depth-of-knowledge question applies: does topical alignment mean students are assessed at comparable cognitive depth across frameworks?

10. **Sequencing and assessment.** This crosswalk compares curriculum frameworks, not enacted curricula or assessment outcomes. Where REAL and CfW show closely sequenced convergence (e.g., on emotional regulation), does REAL's current assessment approach (observation indicators, rubrics) capture the same competency outcomes as CfW's Descriptions of Learning approach? Or are there cases where REAL's content is aligned but its assessment approach is measuring something different?
