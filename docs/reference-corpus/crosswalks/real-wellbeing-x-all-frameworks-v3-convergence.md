## 3. Unique Content

### 3a. Unique to REAL (not substantively addressed in any comparison framework)

- **Focused Attention as a full A–F learning progression** (LT 2.1): No external framework provides a systematic attention-management curriculum from Band A through F. Welsh CfW touches attention awareness at PS3–PS4; CASEL references sustained engagement; but none develop a full progression from sensory attention (Band A) through attentional workflow design (Band F). REAL is the only framework that treats attention management as a teachable, progressively scaffolded competency.
  
- **Wellbeing Science & Neuroscience Literacy as an A–F curriculum** (LT 6.1): No other framework teaches neuroscience (fight/flight/freeze, amygdala-prefrontal interaction, neuroplasticity, HPA axis, habit loops) as curriculum content. RSHE touches mind-body connection at secondary level; Welsh CfW acknowledges physical-emotional links; Circle Solutions has one Year 9 LT on amygdala function. REAL is the only framework with a systematic six-band neuroscience progression.

- **Health & Evidence Literacy as an A–F curriculum** (LT 6.2): Source credibility evaluation (Band A), evidence type analysis (Band C), contested claim adjudication (Band D–E), systematic review interpretation (Band F) — this progression is unique to REAL. RSHE touches health information access at secondary level. No other framework treats critical health literacy as a full multi-year curriculum.

- **Metacognition as an A–F curriculum** (LT 7.1 + LT 7.2): CASEL and Circle Solutions address self-reflection, pattern analysis, and goal-setting at individual bands. REAL uniquely frames metacognition as a structured, auditable practice across all six bands — from "describe one thing I noticed" (Band A) to "construct a personal metacognitive framework" (Band F). The self-direction observable indicators (LT 7.2) provide assessment-ready behavioural anchors absent in all comparison frameworks.

- **Puberty, Sexual Health & Safe Choices from an evidence and rights perspective** (LT 4.2 Bands D–F): While RSHE covers puberty and sexual health propositionally, REAL's Band D–F content uniquely frames sexual health through healthcare navigation, reproductive rights, evidence evaluation, and critical media analysis. This orientation is absent in RSHE and entirely absent in CfW, CASEL, and Circle Solutions.

### 3b. Unique to UK RSHE (not in REAL or other frameworks)

- **Family diversity and different family structures**: Statutory requirement covering single-parent, same-sex, kinship, adoptive, and foster families (End of Primary). No equivalent in REAL, Welsh CfW, CASEL, or Circle Solutions.

- **Digital safety and online risks**: Systematic progression from recognising online abuse (End of Primary) through managing digital footprints, evaluating social media's mental health effects (End of Secondary). REAL only touches online consent (Band C) and digital attention (Band E). Welsh CfW, CASEL, and Circle Solutions have no substantive online safety content.

- **Bullying, prejudice-based bullying, and discrimination**: Systematic coverage from recognising bullying (End of Primary) through illegal discrimination (End of Secondary). Entirely absent from REAL. CASEL and CS address aspects at higher bands but RSHE provides the most systematic treatment.

- **Explicit contraception and STI knowledge**: Factual information on contraception, STIs, and sexual health services. REAL addresses these in LT 4.2 Band D–E but from a risk-assessment and rights perspective; RSHE provides the factual propositional knowledge base separately.

### 3c. Unique to Welsh CfW Health & Well-being AoLE (not in REAL or other frameworks)

- **Physical activity and movement as a curriculum domain** (†Movement Skills Application and Transfer; †Physical Activity Engagement and Motivation): A major component of the Welsh CfW H&W AoLE covering gross motor development, movement skills, sport performance, and physical confidence across all progression steps. Not addressed in any other framework in this crosswalk.

- **Nutrition and food literacy as a standalone progression** (†Understanding Balanced Diet; †Making Informed Food Choices; †Planning and Preparing Nutritious Meals): Welsh CfW develops food literacy from PS1 (diet-health connections) through practical meal planning and preparation skills. REAL integrates nutrition briefly in LT 3.1; RSHE has partial secondary coverage; CASEL and CS have no food literacy content.

### 3d. Unique to CASEL SEL Skills Continuum (not in REAL or other frameworks)

- **Personal identity and self-concept as a full A–F progression**: CASEL's self-awareness competency includes systematic development of self-identity (positive self-identity, self-perception, personal values, cultural identity) from Pre-K through Grade 11-12. REAL has no dedicated identity LT. Welsh CfW addresses identity at PS4–PS5 only. Circle Solutions also covers identity but CASEL provides the most rigorous progression.

- **Growth mindset and self-efficacy as a full A–F progression**: CASEL explicitly teaches growth mindset orientation (†Understanding Growth Mindset, †Enacting Growth Mindset, †Reframing Challenges as Learning Opportunities, †Sustaining Growth-Oriented Self-Perception) across all bands. Not present in REAL, RSHE, or Welsh CfW.

- **Academic goal-setting and achievement planning**: CASEL's self-management competency includes sustained, structured goal-setting and progress monitoring across all bands, connected to academic as well as personal domains. REAL's LT 2.2 addresses decision-making; REAL's LT 7.2 addresses self-direction; but CASEL's explicit goal-setting progression (†Goal Setting and Achievement Planning) is more granular and more explicitly linked to academic persistence.

### 3e. Unique to Circle Solutions SEL Framework (not in REAL or other frameworks)

- **Restorative justice and making amends**: Circle Solutions explicitly addresses harm acknowledgement, restorative processes, making amends (†Engaging in Restorative Processes; †Making Amends for Harm), and analyzing responsibility distribution. REAL addresses relationship repair (LT 1.2 Band D) and conflict resolution (LT 5.1) but not restorative justice as a framing. RSHE references reconciliation; no other framework has a dedicated restorative justice LT.

- **Meaning, purpose, and existential wellbeing at younger bands**: Circle Solutions Year 9 addresses Discussing Life's Meaning (†Discussing Life's Meaning; †Identifying Personal Sources of Meaning) explicitly. REAL only addresses purpose at Band F (LT 5.2). CASEL addresses community contribution at Band D–F. Circle Solutions sequences existential meaning-making significantly earlier.

- **Social justice action planning**: Circle Solutions Year 12 includes †Developing Social Justice Action Plans, explicitly asking students to produce action plans for specific equity issues. This is the most operationalised social justice content across all five frameworks.

## 4. Sequencing Differences

**REAL sequences attention earlier and more explicitly than any other framework.** LT 2.1 begins at Band A with sensory attention and proceeds through strategy evaluation (Band C), attentional design (Band E), and workflow audit (Band F). No comparison framework develops attention as a standalone progression. The planning implication: if REAL students move between frameworks or encounter national assessments, their developed attention-management vocabulary may not match external expectations.

**REAL sequences neuroscience content 2–3 bands earlier than any external framework.** Fight/flight/freeze at Band B; amygdala-prefrontal interaction at Band C; HPA axis at Band D. Circle Solutions addresses amygdala function at Year 9 (Band D/E). RSHE touches mind-body connection at secondary level only. This early scientific framing is a distinctive REAL design choice — PLC should confirm this is intentional (building a foundation for self-regulation understanding) and not an accidental mismatch with developmental expectations.

**REAL sequences health evidence literacy 3+ bands earlier than RSHE.** REAL begins source credibility evaluation at Band A (LT 6.2); RSHE only addresses health information access at End of Secondary (Bands C–E). This suggests REAL treats health literacy as a foundational academic skill; RSHE treats it as a practical secondary competency.

**REAL enters social justice and structural advocacy later than Circle Solutions and later than RSHE.** REAL's systemic advocacy content (LT 1.2 Band F; LT 5.2 Bands E–F) begins at Band E at earliest. Circle Solutions introduces rights-and-responsibilities content at Year 2 (Bands A–B). RSHE covers equality legislation at secondary level (Bands D–E). CASEL addresses community contribution and cultural justice at Band D–F. REAL's late entry into explicit justice-oriented content is a meaningful sequencing difference.

**RSHE sequences online safety from Band A; REAL enters online topics only at Bands C and E.** RSHE introduces online risk recognition from End of Primary (Bands A–C) with systematic coverage through secondary. REAL's LT 4.1 Band C addresses online consent as one aspect of a broader consent LT; LT 2.1 Band E addresses digital environment design as one aspect of attention management. The gap is significant: REAL students in Bands A–B have no explicit online safety learning within the wellbeing framework.

**RSHE and RSHE-aligned curricula expect family diversity content at Band A; REAL has none.** The statutory RSHE requirement for family structures begins at End of Primary (Bands A–C). A REAL School student completing Bands A–D has no structured exposure to family diversity within the wellbeing framework. If REAL School does not cover this elsewhere in the curriculum, this is a compliance-relevant gap for any school operating under RSHE jurisdiction.

## 5. Questions for PLC

1. **The identity gap**: REAL has no dedicated Personal Identity & Self-Concept LT at any band, while CASEL and Circle Solutions develop this A–F. Is the absence intentional (REAL assumes identity is developed through the competency practice itself) or a structural omission? If REAL students are expected to articulate a coherent sense of self by Band F (as suggested by LT 5.2 Band F), where in the curriculum does that development happen?

2. **The growth mindset gap**: CASEL's explicit growth mindset progression (A–F) is absent from REAL. REAL's LT 7.2 (Self-Direction in Practice) covers some related ground dispositionally, but REAL never explicitly names growth mindset as a construct or teaches it as a learnable orientation. Is this a deliberate choice (REAL's metacognitive framing subsumes growth mindset) or an unexamined gap?

3. **Online safety coverage**: RSHE requires systematic digital safety education from Band A; REAL only touches online consent (Band C) and digital attention (Band E). If REAL School students are not receiving online safety education elsewhere in the curriculum, this is a meaningful gap. How is digital safety currently addressed in REAL School's broader programme?

4. **The social justice sequencing decision**: REAL enters systemic advocacy at Band E–F. Circle Solutions begins rights-and-responsibilities content at Band A–B. This is a significant sequencing difference. Is REAL's sequencing a principled developmental choice (structural critique requires prior foundational skills) or does it reflect a gap in the framework's scope at lower bands?

5. **RSHE statutory obligations**: Family diversity (T16), online safety (T17), and bullying/discrimination (T18) are all statutory RSHE requirements that have no REAL equivalent. For any school operating under UK RSHE jurisdiction, this raises a question: how does REAL's wellbeing framework complement or substitute for statutory RSHE content? If REAL students take RSHE alongside REAL Wellbeing, there may be redundancy in T01, T02, T08, T09; but for T16, T17, T18 there is complementarity, not redundancy.

6. **The neuroscience sequencing decision**: REAL introduces the amygdala and prefrontal cortex at Band C. No other framework in this crosswalk does this before Band D/E. Is this sequencing grounded in a specific learning science rationale? If so, what evidence supports it? If not, is it worth reviewing against developmental psychology literature on children's capacity for explicit neuroscience reasoning.

7. **Physical health: REAL vs. Welsh CfW**: Welsh CfW H&W has an entire major thread on physical activity and movement (T19) that is absent from REAL. REAL covers health habits (T06) with sleep, food, and movement integrated, but does not develop movement as a standalone curriculum domain. Is this appropriate given REAL's context (is movement addressed in PE?), or is there a gap in REAL's physical wellbeing coverage?

8. **REAL's unique strengths — are they defensible?**: T12 (Wellbeing Science), T13 (Health Evidence Literacy), and T14 (Metacognition) are substantially unique to REAL across A–F. These represent a distinctive pedagogical bet: that systematic intellectual engagement with the science of wellbeing is more durable than procedural skill development alone. Is there any planned mechanism to evaluate whether this bet is paying off — are REAL students developing meaningfully different wellbeing outcomes compared to frameworks without these components?

9. **RSHE Band F gap**: UK RSHE has no post-16 (Band F) content by design — it is a pre-16 statutory curriculum. REAL Bands E–F address content (systemic empathy, mature resilience, sustained civic action) that RSHE does not reach. For a school serving students through Band F, REAL adds clear post-RSHE content. Is REAL School providing explicit Band F wellbeing learning that is currently invisible in any statutory or widely-used external framework?

10. **The social justice sequencing paradox**: REAL's Band F empathy LT (LT 1.2 Band F) includes systemic advocacy as its pinnacle — recognising structural factors, adjusting empathic response, advocating for systemic change. This is placed inside an empathy LT rather than a community engagement or justice LT. Circle Solutions places social justice action as a standalone Year 12 construct (†Developing Social Justice Action Plans). Should REAL consider whether its structural-critique and advocacy content would be better served by an explicit community and justice LT rather than being embedded as the terminal descriptor of an empathy LT?
