# Crosswalk — REAL Wellbeing × UK Statutory RSHE × Welsh CfW × CASEL SEL × Circle Solutions

**Scope**: REAL Bands A–F (all six bands). **Compared frameworks**: REAL School Budapest Wellbeing Framework (band-tagged v1, 2026-04); UK DfE Statutory RSHE, July 2025 (band-tagged v1, 2026-04); Welsh Curriculum for Wales — Health and Well-being AoLE, Descriptions of Learning per Progression Step (band-tagged v2, 2026-04); CASEL SEL Skills Continuum, January 2023 (band-tagged v1, 2026-04); Circle Solutions SEL Framework — Cowie & Myers (2016), Assessment Framework observable indicators for Year 2, Year 6, Year 9, Year 12 (band-tagged v1, 2026-04).

**PLC context**: REAL School Budapest wellbeing team reviewing coverage and sequencing of the REAL wellbeing framework across bands A–F against four external frameworks — UK statutory RSHE, Welsh CfW Health & Wellbeing, CASEL SEL Skills Continuum, and the Circle Solutions SEL Assessment Framework — in preparation for PLC review and framework finalisation.

---

**Note on upstream band ambiguity**: UK RSHE content is tagged to either "End of Primary" (→ REAL Bands A–C, all items ambiguous across the full primary phase) or "End of Secondary" (→ REAL Bands C–E, all items ambiguous across the full secondary phase). Welsh CfW Progression Step 1 maps unambiguously to Band A; all other PS mappings carry a two-band span: PS2→[B,C], PS3→[C,D], PS4→[D,E], PS5→[E,F]. CASEL grade bands map cleanly to REAL bands with high confidence (Pre-K→A pre-band; K-1→A; 2-3→B; 4-5→C; 6-8→D; 9-10→E; 11-12→F). Circle Solutions year levels carry an ambiguity flag: Year 2→[A,B] (age 7), Year 6→C (age 10-11), Year 9→[D,E] (age 13-14), Year 12→F (age 16-17). Rows in the tables below preserve all candidate bands; band-gap calculations use midpoints of ambiguous ranges.

**Note on Band E/F REAL content**: The 28 items in REAL Bands E and F were newly authored in session REAL-2a (2026-04-20) and have not yet been through full REAL School teacher review. They appear in the tables below where relevant and are marked *(draft — teacher review pending)*.

**Note on CASEL Band F**: CASEL Grade Band 11-12 (→ REAL Band F) produced 0 KUD items and 0 LTs due to a pipeline classification halt (5 content blocks were `classification_unreliable` — 3/3 LLM runs disagreed on knowledge type for complex senior-level items). Band F has no CASEL crosswalk rows below. The source content exists in `docs/reference-corpus/casel-sel-continuum/source.md` and can be manually classified if needed.

**Note on Circle Solutions source structure**: Circle Solutions has 4 year levels covering Bands A/B, C, D/E, and F. Bands B and E are inferred by the two-year-level span (Year 2 age-7 → [A,B]; Year 9 age 13-14 → [D,E]). This is a sparse source structure relative to REAL's 6-band framework.

**Note on voice conventions**: REAL content appears in REAL's "I can / Students can" voice. RSHE content appears in the statutory propositional register. Welsh CfW content appears in its "I can / I have" first-person learner voice. CASEL content alternates between "I can" items (T1/T2 knowledge) and dispositional orientation statements (T3). Circle Solutions content appears as observable assessment indicators in third-person performance voice. No statements have been rewritten or paraphrased; all quotations are verbatim from the band-tagged sources.

## 3. Unique Content

Content present in only one framework within the scope of this five-framework comparison.

### REAL only (no parallel in UK RSHE, Welsh CfW, CASEL, or Circle Solutions)

**Competency 6 — Wellbeing Science & Literacy (LT 6.1 Brain, Body & Wellbeing Science, all bands)**

- Band A: "I can name body signals that tell me something about how I feel, and describe what sleep and food do for my body and brain."
- Band B: "I can explain what happens in my body during a fight, flight, or freeze response, and describe the three parts of the habit loop."
- Band C: "I can explain how the amygdala and prefrontal cortex interact during a stressful moment, explain what neuroplasticity means, and describe how the habit loop explains behaviour change at the level of mechanism."
- Band D: "I can use accurate neuroscience vocabulary to explain how stress, emotion, attention, and habit interact as a system, and describe the role of the HPA axis in the stress response."
- Band E: "I can apply my understanding of the stress-emotion-attention-habit system to evaluate a wellbeing intervention — explaining its proposed mechanism, what the evidence suggests about its effectiveness, and what it would and would not change." *(draft — teacher review pending)*
- Band F: "I can critically evaluate a contested claim in wellbeing science — identifying where the evidence is strong, where it is uncertain, and where popular accounts have oversimplified or misrepresented the research — and form a reasoned position of my own." *(draft — teacher review pending)*

*Partial exception*: Circle Solutions Year 9 (Band D/E) includes "Knows the role of the amygdala and how it can hijack 'thinking'" — this is the only parallel to REAL's neuroscience strand across all four external frameworks, and it appears at Band D/E. REAL's neuroscience strand begins at Band A and runs to Band F; Circle Solutions has one touchpoint at Band D/E. Neither RSHE, CfW, nor CASEL address neuroscience as a curricular domain.

**Competency 6 — Wellbeing Science & Literacy (LT 6.2 Health Information Literacy, all bands)**

- Band C: "I can analyse a health claim by examining the type of evidence it uses and identifying whether the source has a reason to be biased."
- Band D: "I can evaluate a contested health claim by comparing the quality of evidence on each side, identifying the strongest and weakest arguments, and justifying my conclusion."
- Band E: "I can assess the strength of an evidence base for a health claim — not just whether individual sources are trustworthy, but whether the cumulative research warrants the confidence placed in the claim — and identify the commercial or institutional interests that may have shaped the research agenda." *(draft — teacher review pending)*
- Band F: "I can read and interpret a systematic review at an introductory level, trace how a policy recommendation was derived from an evidence base, and evaluate whether a specific guideline is well-supported or contested." *(draft — teacher review pending)*

None of the four external frameworks treat health information literacy as a standalone strand progressing from source evaluation (Band C) to systematic review interpretation (Band F).

**Competency 7 — Metacognitive Self-Direction (LT 7.1 Pattern Analysis and LT 7.2 Self-Direction in Practice, all bands)**

- LT 7.1 Band A: "I can describe one thing I noticed about how I reacted to a situation and say what I might do differently next time."
- LT 7.2 Band B: "Refers back to a strategy they have previously found helpful, without being prompted."
- LT 7.1 Band C: "I can analyse a pattern across more than one situation, explain what drives it, and describe a specific adjustment I have made or will make."
- LT 7.2 Band D: "Articulates a specific adjustment they have made to their thinking or behaviour and describes its effect across more than one domain. In novel or challenging situations, applies a self-awareness strategy without an adult prompt."
- LT 7.1 Band E: "I can apply a structured metacognitive protocol to a high-stakes academic or personal challenge — mapping my typical patterns, identifying the sustaining conditions, designing a specific intervention, tracking its effects, and iterating based on results." *(draft — teacher review pending)*
- LT 7.2 Band F: "Demonstrates a stable, articulated metacognitive identity — a consistent pattern of how they learn, reflect, and adjust — that they can describe to others and that persists across highly novel, high-stakes, or unfamiliar contexts without any scaffolding." *(draft — teacher review pending)*

None of the four external frameworks have an explicit metacognitive self-direction strand. CASEL's goal-setting items (Band A–E) are the closest parallel but address concrete goal formation, not the recursive pattern-recognition and identity-level consolidation REAL aims at.

**Competency 2 — Attention & Reflective Practices (LT 2.1 Focused Attention & Strategy, Bands E–F)**

## 4. Sequencing Differences

**Emotional regulation and self-awareness.** All five frameworks sequence emotion regulation from simple to complex, but with different grain and pacing. REAL has a 6-band explicit progression from naming one feeling (Band A) to articulating a personal regulation system and teaching it to others (Band F). Welsh CfW has a parallel 5-step progression, tracking closely with REAL. CASEL has a 5-band progression (Pre-K/K-1 through Grade 9-10) that closely mirrors REAL's sequencing with an equivalent grain size — the two frameworks are the most closely aligned on regulation sequencing of any pair in this crosswalk. Circle Solutions' 4-year-level structure maps regulation content at Year 2, 6, 9, and 12, with less fine-grained developmental staging between levels. UK RSHE has no fine-grained regulation progression — regulation content appears only in aggregate at End of Primary and End of Secondary.

**Consent and body autonomy.** REAL introduces body ownership and trusted adults at Band A (LT 4.1), moves to boundary-setting and consent skills at Band B, generalises consent online at Band C, addresses power and peer pressure at Band D, applies legal consent knowledge at Band E, and reaches peer-education capacity at Band F. UK RSHE introduces body rights and boundaries at End of Primary (A–C) and legal/ethical consent at End of Secondary (C–E) — a 2-phase approach. CASEL has no consent or body-autonomy strand. Circle Solutions addresses assertiveness from Year 2 and peer-pressure resistance at Year 12, but has no explicit consent progression. A notable difference: RSHE places grooming awareness firmly in the primary phase (A–C), while REAL's explicit treatment of grooming and exploitation risk appears at Band E (LT 4.1) — a 2-band gap.

**Neuroscience education.** REAL begins introducing body-brain connections at Band A and sequences amygdala-PFC interaction at Band C-D, escalating to HPA axis and stress system (Band D), wellbeing intervention evaluation (Band E), and contested claims (Band F). Circle Solutions introduces amygdala function at Year 9 (Band D/E) — later than REAL's Band C-D introduction, and at a simpler grain (recognition without mechanism). CASEL, RSHE, and CfW have no neuroscience strand. REAL's neuroscience progression is the most analytically ambitious element of its framework and is unique in scope and depth across all five frameworks.

**Decision-making.** REAL's 6-band decision-making progression (LT 2.2) moves from single-observation reflection (Band A) to values-anchored choice (Band C) to bias identification and epistemic humility (Band E) to reasoned positions under irreducible uncertainty (Band F). CASEL runs the most closely parallel progression: from consequence recognition (Grade 2-3) to structured decision-making models with impact analysis (Grade 6-8) to evidence-gathering and ethical reasoning (Grade 9-10). Welsh CfW's WM3 is also closely aligned, with CfW being slightly more collective-minded (group decisions appear early) and REAL more individually epistemological (bias and reasoning quality explicit from Band D). Circle Solutions addresses decision-making through its goal-setting (Dim 7) and conflict-resolution (Dim 9) strands rather than a dedicated decision-making progression. RSHE embeds decision-making in relationships content.

**Repair and restoration.** Circle Solutions introduces intentional repair concepts at Year 2 (Band A/B): "Checks whether a hurt was an accident or deliberate." REAL's explicit relationship repair skill appears at Band D (LT 1.2), approximately 3 bands later. This represents the largest sequencing gap in the crosswalk. Circle Solutions' early-years repair language may offer a complementary scaffold for REAL, particularly given that REAL students arrive at the school across bands. CASEL, RSHE, and CfW all sequence repair later than Circle Solutions, but earlier than REAL (CASEL Grade 2-3 has friendship behaviors; CfW PS3 has conflict management).

**Help-seeking and mental health support.** REAL introduces the concept of a named trusted adult at Band A (LT 3.2) and builds to accessing confidential health services at Band E. CASEL begins help-seeking at Pre-K (safety resources, identifying community helpers) and escalates to seeking appropriate support for academic and personal challenges through Band E. Both REAL and CASEL sequence help-seeking from Band A, and their parallel progression is closer than with RSHE or CfW. Welsh CfW's explicit mental-health help-seeking framing appears at PS3 (Bands C–D), 2 bands later than REAL's introduction. Circle Solutions does not have a dedicated help-seeking strand until Year 12 (Dim 2: "Can tell the difference between being sad and being depressed. Knows who to seek out for support when they are struggling."), which is notably late relative to REAL, CASEL, and CfW.

**Community engagement and civic purpose.** REAL sequences community contribution from Band A (classroom job, LT 5.2) through documented project impact (Band D) to leading sustained initiatives (Band E) to articulating purpose and legacy (Band F). CASEL's parallel progression goes from collaborative idea-sharing (K-1) through community problem-solving (Grade 4-5) to active community contribution (Grade 6-8) and community well-being orientation (Grade 9-10). The two frameworks are closely sequenced on this strand. Circle Solutions addresses community through Dim 11 (ethics, integrity, human rights) at Year 9 and Year 12 with social justice action projects. Welsh CfW addresses community through pro-social advocacy at PS5. RSHE has no explicit community engagement strand.

## 5. Questions for PLC

1. **Grooming and exploitation timing.** RSHE treats grooming awareness as a primary-phase safeguarding topic (End of Primary, Bands A–C), while REAL's explicit treatment of "coercion, grooming, or exploitation risk" appears at Band E (LT 4.1). Neither CASEL nor Circle Solutions address grooming. Should REAL introduce a lighter-touch, age-appropriate version of this content at Bands B or C? What would a Band B version of grooming-awareness look like in REAL's dispositional voice?

2. **Online image-sharing law.** UK RSHE dedicates multiple items at End of Secondary (Bands C–E) to the criminal law around indecent images, sextortion, and AI-generated imagery. None of CASEL, Circle Solutions, or Welsh CfW address these as distinct curricular topics. REAL does not address them either. Given that REAL students are of secondary age (Bands C–F), does the team believe this legal knowledge is adequately addressed through REAL's consent strand (LT 4.1) or through pastoral/tutor systems — or is there a gap in REAL's coverage?

3. **Meaning and flourishing.** Circle Solutions' Dimension 12 (Meaning and spirituality) runs from Year 2 through Year 12 with explicit assessable indicators for what makes life go well — "what would make someone happy always" (Year 2), choosing a life role model (Year 6), "a good life" discussion (Year 9), and a personal values statement (Year 12). REAL has no equivalent strand. Does the wellbeing team believe that flourishing — not just regulation, relationships, and community — should be an explicit curricular object? If so, should it be added to REAL's framework, or is it addressed implicitly through LT 5.2 (purpose) and LT 2.2 (values)?

4. **Repair and restoration sequencing.** Circle Solutions introduces intentional repair language at Year 2 (Band A/B) — checking whether a hurt was an accident, talking about "inside" and "outside" hurts. REAL's explicit relationship repair skill appears at Band D. Is the approximately 3-band gap intentional (REAL prioritises naming and regulating feelings first) or is it an oversight? Should Bands A-C include earlier repair language, even if the full repair skill is at Band D?

5. **Self-advocacy as a named strand.** CASEL explicitly names and progressively develops self-advocacy from Grade 2-3 through Grade 9-10. REAL addresses help-seeking (LT 3.2) and communicating needs (LT 5.1) but does not name self-advocacy as a curricular object. Given that REAL students include international students who may need to navigate unfamiliar institutional systems, should REAL add a self-advocacy thread to LT 3.2 or LT 5.1?

6. **Growth mindset.** CASEL teaches growth mindset explicitly at Grade Band 4-5 (Band C): students learn what it is and enact it as an orientation. REAL has a metacognitive progression (LT 7.1/7.2) that covers related territory at a deeper level — but doesn't name growth mindset as a concept students should understand. Given the prevalence of growth mindset language in educational practice, does REAL want to name it explicitly at Band C, or does the team believe LT 7.1/7.2 covers the underlying construct more rigorously?

7. **Neuroscience as distinctive content.** LT 6.1 (Brain, Body & Wellbeing Science) has a small partial parallel only in Circle Solutions (amygdala at Year 9). It has no parallel in RSHE, CfW, or CASEL. REAL's neuroscience strand is the most distinctive element of the framework in this crosswalk. Is this distinctiveness intentional and worth protecting as a claim about what a REAL wellbeing education uniquely offers?

8. **Metacognition and its placement.** LT 7.1 and LT 7.2 (Metacognitive Self-Direction) have no parallel in any of the four external frameworks. They are the most pedagogically unusual feature of REAL at secondary level. The PLC should ask: is metacognitive self-direction a wellbeing competency, a learning-skills competency, or both — and how does REAL currently assess it?

9. **Circle Solutions as a pedagogical model.** Circle Solutions is not just a curriculum framework — it is a structured pedagogical approach (circle process, collaborative indicators). Several of its observable indicators describe pedagogical moments ("in a paired discussion," "joins in a class project") rather than curriculum outcomes. Does the wellbeing team use Circle Solutions as a delivery method at REAL? If so, the crosswalk highlights where CASEL and Circle Solutions converge well with REAL outcomes and could inform curriculum delivery as well as content design.

10. **CASEL-REAL alignment for international benchmark.** CASEL is the most internationally recognised SEL framework in the data set. The crosswalk shows strong convergence between REAL and CASEL across Bands A–E on Competencies 1, 2 (decision-making), and 5 (community). Where REAL diverges from CASEL (no consent strand, no named growth mindset, no self-advocacy strand) could be presented as intentional distinctions or as gaps depending on the audience. Is there value in developing a REAL-to-CASEL alignment document for external stakeholders (accreditors, partner schools), distinct from this internal PLC crosswalk?

11. **Physical movement and sport.** Welsh CfW has a full progression strand on physical movement and sport (WM1) that has no parallel in REAL or in any other framework in this comparison. If REAL's Competency 3 (Physical Wellbeing & Self-Care) is intended to address physical health holistically, should it include a movement or sport element? Or does REAL's school structure mean physical education is handled separately?

12. **Newly authored Band E/F content.** Twenty-eight items in REAL Bands E and F were authored in April 2026 and have not yet been reviewed by REAL teachers. The crosswalk shows high-confidence convergence between several Band E/F items and CASEL Grade 9-10 and Welsh CfW PS5 content — providing external validity for the direction of travel, if not for the specific wording. PLC should review all Band E/F items, with particular attention to Competency 4 (LT 4.1/4.2 at Bands E/F) and Competency 6 (LT 6.1/6.2 at Bands E/F), where the external frameworks diverge most sharply from REAL's framing.
